Lesson 13. Hinder the one-party state.



However [political parties] may now and then answer popular ends,

they are likely in the course of time and things to become potent engines,

by which cunning,

ambitious,

and unprincipled men

will be enabled to subvert

the power of the people

and to usurp for themselves the reins of government.

— George Washington, Farewell Address (1796)
